Story

Engineering is how I practice care, discipline, and clarity.

Levio began as a late-night attempt to steady my grandmother’s Parkinson’s therapy from three thousand miles away. That project reshaped how I work: start from a lived story, prove the system formally, and wire telemetry so I know when reality disagrees with intent. Every build since then follows the same pattern—careful abstractions, measurable results, and a bias toward tools that people can trust.

Operating principles

  • Structure engagements as systems: crisp interfaces, testable units, intentional abstractions.
  • Balance velocity with rigor—proofs, experiments, and postmortems are part of the deliverable.
  • Ship with observability and privacy from the first commit instead of retrofitting guardrails.
  • Write clear docs so future teammates can extend the work without restarting the investigation.
