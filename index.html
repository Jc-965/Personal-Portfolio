Story

Engineering is how I practice care, discipline, and clarity.

My story starts in my grandmother’s kitchen. Years later, Parkinson’s shook the hands that taught me patience, and Levio became my way to help from three thousand miles away. Since then I’ve chased the idea that software should feel steady and human: design from empathy, implement with rigor, and keep instrumentation close so I know when to adjust.

Operating principles

  • Structure work like code: sharp interfaces, testable units, intentional abstractions.
  • Pair fast loops with careful math—proofs, experiments, and postmortems included.
  • Ship with observability and privacy from the first commit, not as a retrofit.
  • Invest in documentation so future teammates inherit context, not guesswork.
